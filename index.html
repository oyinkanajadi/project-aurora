Executive Summary

A digital transformation built around patient access

Aurora Health Group — a mid-sized UK private healthcare provider — faced growing patient demand alongside an increasingly strained booking operation. Manual processes, siloed data, and no automated communications were creating friction at every touchpoint.

Project Aurora was commissioned to redesign the patient booking journey from the ground up: replacing manual scheduling with an integrated digital platform, automating appointment reminders, and introducing real-time capacity visibility for operational teams.

The result was a measurably more efficient, patient-centred access model that reduced did-not-attends (DNAs), cut response times, and freed clinical administration staff from time-consuming manual tasks.

Pilot conducted across two sites over a 6-week period with cross-functional stakeholder involvement spanning clinical operations, IT, and patient-facing services.

Overview

The organisation & the challenge

Aurora Health Group delivers outpatient and specialist services across multiple UK sites. Growing referral volumes were exposing the limits of a manual, phone-led booking operation.

Organisation

Mid-sized UK private healthcare provider operating outpatient and specialist clinics.

Challenge

Rising demand met with manual processes, no system integration, and poor patient communication.

Goal

Improve patient access, reduce DNAs, and deliver a scalable digital booking capability.

Process Analysis

AS-IS vs TO-BE: the booking journey

Stakeholder workshops and process observation uncovered significant manual effort and lost demand in the current state. The redesigned journey automates the high-volume, low-value steps so staff can focus on complex cases.

⚠️ AS-IS — Current State
1
Patient contacts clinic by phone
2
Admin manually checks availability
3
Appointment confirmed verbally
4
Manual data entry into system
5
No reminder — patient may not attend
  • Manual scheduling with no real-time availability
  • No reminder workflow — high DNA rate
  • Siloed data across systems
  • Lost demand through missed calls and call-backs
✅ TO-BE — Future State
1
Patient self-books via portal or phone IVR
2
Real-time slot selection from integrated calendar
3
Automated confirmation sent via SMS/email
4
Auto-reminder 48h and 24h before appointment
5
Outcome logged — reporting updated in real time
  • Integrated digital booking with live capacity
  • Automated SMS/email reminders reduce DNAs
  • Single data source eliminates duplication
  • Demand captured 24/7 without staff intervention
Pain Points Identified

Root causes driving inefficiency

Discovered through stakeholder interviews, process observation, and data analysis across three sites.

📞

Missed Demand

Unanswered calls and no callback mechanism meant patients were lost before a booking was even attempted.

🔀

Manual Coordination

Admins coordinating across multiple systems by phone and spreadsheet, creating delays and errors.

🔭

No Capacity Visibility

Operational teams lacked a real-time view of slot availability, leading to over and under-booking.

🔔

No Reminders

Patients received no automated reminders, resulting in a 25% did-not-attend rate and wasted clinical time.

🗂️

Duplicate Data

Patient information entered separately into multiple disconnected systems, risking inaccuracy and duplication.

Conclusion

A foundation for scalable, patient-centred access

Project Aurora demonstrated that targeted process redesign — backed by strong analysis and stakeholder engagement — can deliver meaningful operational improvement without requiring large-scale clinical transformation.

By replacing manual coordination with integrated digital tools, Aurora Health Group now has a scalable, data-driven access model. The pilot results provide a clear business case for full rollout across all sites, with integration risk identified as the primary dependency to resolve.
