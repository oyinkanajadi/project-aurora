About

Operational transformation — from diagnosis to delivery.

I work at the intersection of continuous improvement and project management. My focus is on identifying what is actually causing operational inefficiency — not just the surface symptoms — and then designing and delivering changes that hold.

That means process mapping, root cause analysis, and stakeholder alignment before a single solution is proposed. And it means rigorous delivery management, clear governance, and measurable KPIs to ensure that what gets built actually gets used.

I've worked across patient access, enterprise system implementation, and data & reporting — each requiring a different application of CI and PM principles, and each resulting in measurable operational improvement.

How I Work

A structured approach to every engagement.

01

Diagnose before designing

Every engagement starts with deep-dive process observation, stakeholder interviews, and root cause analysis. Solutions only follow once the real problem is understood — not assumed.

02

Design for adoption, not just delivery

A well-designed process that nobody uses is a failed project. Change management, clear communication, and user involvement are built into every stage — not added at the end.

03

Measure what matters

Every project is anchored to a KPI framework defined at the start. Outcomes are tracked against baselines — so there is always an honest answer to the question: did it work?
