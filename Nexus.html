Executive Summary

Stabilising a failing implementation before it became a crisis

Project Nexus was commissioned to implement an enterprise procurement system — integrating a purchase order (PO) platform with the organisation's existing ERP — across multiple business units with differing approval structures and financial processes. What began as a planned rollout quickly encountered significant delivery challenges: integration failures between systems, incorrect approval routing, supplier onboarding delays, and high operational disruption.

The engagement shifted from a straightforward implementation to an active stabilisation. The priority became resolving critical defects, restoring stakeholder confidence, and establishing a controlled, phased adoption pathway that reduced risk and gave operational teams the time to adapt.

Success here was not measured in features delivered, but in systems stabilised, processes defined, and people equipped to work effectively in the new environment.

Delivered across multiple business units with cross-functional involvement spanning procurement, IT, operations, and third-party suppliers.

Context

The organisation & the environment

A large multi-site organisation transitioning from manual procurement processes to a fully digital PO workflow — a high-stakes change with significant financial and operational risk.

The System

A specialist PO platform integrated with the organisation's existing ERP. Two complex enterprise systems required to work in sync across business units.

The Environment

Multiple business units with different approval hierarchies, chart of accounts structures, and varying levels of digital readiness.

The Risk

High financial exposure if transactions were routed incorrectly or rejected — incorrect financial data handling could cause material reporting errors.

Core Challenges

What was blocking progress

Five interconnected failure modes emerged during the initial rollout phase, each requiring a distinct resolution approach.

🔀

Approval Routing Failures

Transactions were routing to incorrect approvers due to misconfigured approval hierarchies in the platform, creating bottlenecks and compliance risk.

🔗

Financial Data Alignment Issues

Integration between the platform and the ERP was producing misaligned financial data, risking reporting integrity across business units.

🚚

Supplier Onboarding Delays

Key suppliers were not onboarded to the portal in time, forcing manual workarounds and undermining the business case for digitisation.

🔐

User Access & Authentication Issues

System access configuration failures prevented a significant proportion of users from logging in during go-live, impacting adoption and trust in the platform.

😕

User Confusion & Resistance

Insufficient change management ahead of go-live left users without confidence in the new system, driving workarounds and shadow processes.

Delivery Approach

Shifting from rollout to stabilisation

Recognising that a full go-live was not viable in the current state, the delivery approach was redesigned to prioritise stability over speed.

🔄
Controlled Pilot

Moved from full entity rollout to a controlled pilot, limiting exposure while issues were resolved.

🐛
Structured Issue Tracking

Introduced a centralised issue tracking tool, replacing ad-hoc email chains and spreadsheets with a structured log visible to all teams.

🎯
Defect Triage

Implemented a severity-based triage process to prioritise critical defects blocking adoption over cosmetic issues.

🏛️
Governance Reset

Re-established a cross-functional steering forum with clear decision rights and escalation paths.

Key Interventions

What made the difference

01

Centralised Issue Control

All defects, blockers, and open questions consolidated into a single centralised tracking system with owners, priorities, and resolution targets assigned.

Impact: Eliminated duplication, created shared visibility across teams, and enabled weekly resolution reporting to senior stakeholders.
02

Stakeholder Realignment

Re-established governance across procurement, IT, operations, and external suppliers. Clarified roles, decision rights, and escalation paths that had become blurred during go-live.

Impact: Reduced escalation noise, accelerated supplier responses, and restored confidence among operational leads.
03

Guided Adoption Programme

Replaced failed formal training with a flexible drop-in session model and concise job aids — step-by-step visual guides tailored to each user group's workflow.

Impact: Engagement rates improved week-on-week. Users reported greater confidence and fewer workarounds within four weeks.
04

SOP & Process Definition

Documented standard operating procedures for key PO workflows — raising, approving, and querying — ensuring consistency across entities and reducing user error rates.

Impact: Provided a reference baseline for onboarding new users and auditing process adherence post-rollout.

Key Insight

What this project taught

Successful delivery was not achieved through system implementation alone — but through stabilising people, process, and governance alongside technology.

The technical fix was only part of the solution. The lasting improvement came from restoring trust, clarity, and structure around the change.
