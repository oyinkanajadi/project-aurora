Executive Summary

From fragmented data to a foundation for better decisions

Project Atlas was initiated in response to a growing gap between the volume of data available to the organisation and its ability to use that data meaningfully. Operational teams were producing reports manually, pulling from disconnected systems, and applying inconsistent definitions to the same metrics — resulting in conflicting figures, eroded trust in reporting, and leadership teams making decisions without a reliable performance baseline.

The work went beyond simply improving dashboards. It involved redesigning how data was defined, owned, structured, and used across functions. A standardised KPI framework was introduced, a single source of truth principle was established, and a reporting cadence was put in place to shift the organisation's relationship with performance data from reactive and ad hoc to structured and forward-looking.

The result was a measurable improvement in decision-making confidence, a significant reduction in manual reporting effort, and a shared language around performance that had previously been absent.

Delivered across a multi-functional organisation with cross-functional involvement spanning operations, finance, and senior leadership.

Context

The organisation & the data landscape

A multi-functional organisation with data spread across multiple disconnected systems — each team maintaining its own version of performance metrics with no shared governance or agreed definitions.

The Environment

Multiple operational functions each managing their own reporting in isolation, using different tools, data extracts, and KPI definitions for the same metrics.

The Problem

No single source of truth, heavy reliance on manual processes, and a lack of agreed metric definitions meant leadership could not trust or act confidently on performance data.

The Risk

Delayed and poorly informed decisions, eroded stakeholder trust in reporting, and an inability to identify performance trends before they became operational problems.

Core Challenges

What was holding performance back

Five interconnected data and reporting problems that compounded each other and required a systemic, not tactical, response.

🗂️

Fragmented Data Sources

Performance data was spread across multiple systems and manual extracts, with no consistent method for combining or reconciling it.

⚙️

Manual Reporting Processes

Reports were produced by hand, consuming significant time and introducing errors at every stage of compilation and distribution.

📏

Inconsistent KPI Definitions

The same metrics were calculated differently across teams, making cross-functional comparisons unreliable and leadership conversations difficult.

🔭

Limited Leadership Visibility

Without a consolidated view, senior leaders were working from incomplete or outdated information, limiting their ability to intervene early on emerging issues.

🔁

Reactive Decision-Making

Reporting cadences were slow and retrospective — by the time data reached decision-makers, the window for timely action had often passed.

State Change

Where we started vs. where we landed

The transformation was not just technical — it required changing how people thought about and engaged with performance data across the organisation.

⚠️ Before

  • Multiple teams, multiple definitions of the same KPIs
  • Reports built manually in spreadsheets, prone to error
  • Data pulled from different systems with no reconciliation
  • Leadership received reports too late to act on trends
  • No governance over how data was captured or used
  • Low confidence in performance figures across the board

✅ After

  • Agreed KPI definitions documented and adopted organisation-wide
  • Structured dashboard-based reporting replacing manual compilation
  • Single source of truth established with clear data ownership
  • Regular reporting cadence giving leadership timely visibility
  • Data governance framework with defined rules and responsibilities
  • Increased trust in performance data enabling confident decisions
Key Interventions

What drove the transformation

01

Standardised KPI Framework

Worked with teams across functions to define, agree, and document a consistent set of performance metrics — establishing what each KPI meant, how it was calculated, and who owned it.

Impact: Eliminated conflicting figures in leadership reporting and created a shared performance language across the organisation.
02

Single Source of Truth

Identified and agreed the authoritative data sources for each metric, replacing the proliferation of parallel extracts and manual spreadsheets with a single, governed reporting layer.

Impact: Significantly reduced time spent reconciling conflicting data and increased stakeholder confidence in reported figures.
03

Dashboard-Based Reporting

Designed and introduced a structured dashboard reporting approach to replace manual report production, providing leadership with clear, accessible, and timely performance views.

Impact: Reduced manual reporting effort substantially and gave leadership the real-time visibility needed for proactive decision-making.
04

Data Governance Framework

Established clear governance rules covering data capture, ownership, access, and quality — ensuring the improvements made were sustainable and not reliant on individual effort to maintain.

Impact: Created an organisational foundation for ongoing data quality and enabled future reporting improvements to build on solid ground.
05

Reporting Cadence Design

Introduced a structured performance review cadence — defining when reports were produced, who received them, and how they were used in decision-making forums.

Impact: Shifted the organisation's engagement with data from ad hoc and reactive to scheduled and forward-looking.

Key Insight

What this project taught

Better decisions are not driven by more data — but by clearer, trusted, and aligned data.

The organisations with the best-performing reporting cultures are rarely the ones with the most data. They are the ones that have agreed on what matters, defined it precisely, and made it visible at the right time.
